DAWA trial

Digital Antibiotic stewardship and training for Wahudumu wa Afya (DAWA)

DAWA is a cluster-randomized trial evaluating the effectiveness of a digital clinical decision support system, training and mentorship to reduce antibiotic prescribing at primary care health facilities on Unguja and Pemba islands, Zanzibar, Tanzania.

Background

Inappropriate antibiotic prescribing is widespread in primary care settings in low- and middle-income countries, contributing to bacterial antimicrobial resistance (AMR), and excess morbidity and mortality.

Digital clinical decision support systems (CDSS) integrated within electronic medical record (EMR) systems have shown potential to improve antibiotic stewardship, yet existing tools have important limitations: most target only children under five years of age, and many are not embedded in the health facilities’ digital infrastructure.

The DAWA project tests a comprehensive antibiotic stewardship intervention — combining digital clinical decision support integrated within the Zanzibar Electronic Medical Record (ZanEMR) system with targeted training and monthly peer education meetings — at primary care dispensaries on Unguja and Pemba islands, Zanzibar.

Study Overview

Full title A cluster-randomized trial to assess the effectiveness of a digital clinical decision support system, training and mentorship to reduce antibiotic prescribing at primary care health facilities in Tanzania
Short title Digital Antibiotic stewardship and Training for Wahudumu wa Afya (DAWA)
Setting Public dispensaries, Unguja and Pemba islands, Zanzibar, Tanzania
Design Three-arm parallel-group cluster randomized controlled trial
Sample size 5,850 patients (1,950 per arm)
Estimated first enrolment 1 November 2026
Registry To be registered on ClinicalTrials.gov
Sponsor Centre for Primary Care and Public Health (Unisanté), University of Lausanne, Switzerland

Primary Objective

To evaluate the effectiveness of two antibiotic stewardship interventions — both incorporating clinical decision support integrated within an electronic medical record system, and one with an additional monthly peer education component — to reduce antibiotic prescribing for acute infections at primary care health facilities on Unguja and Pemba islands, Zanzibar, Tanzania.

Study Arms

  • Arm 1 (Multifaceted Intervention): ZanEMR-AS (digital clinical decision support integrated within ZanEMR) with initial group training, supported mentorship visits, and monthly Antibiotic Stewardship Club meetings.

  • Arm 2 (Digital Support Alone): ZanEMR-AS with initial group training and supported mentorship visits.

  • Arm 3 (Usual Care): Standard ZanEMR (no antibiotic stewardship features).

The DAWA project is a collaboration between the Centre for Primary Care and Public Health (Unisanté), University of Lausanne; the Ifakara Health Institute (IHI), Tanzania; and the Zanzibar Ministry of Health. It is funded by the Swiss National Science Foundation (SNSF).

Study Location

The DAWA trial takes place at public dispensaries on Unguja and Pemba islands, Zanzibar, an archipelago off the east coast of Tanzania.
